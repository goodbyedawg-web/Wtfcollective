# Psychological Profiles of Serial Killers: Patterns and Predictors

Serial killers aren't born—they're shaped by a complex interplay of psychological factors. Understanding these profiles helps us identify warning signs and prevent future tragedies.

## Common Psychological Traits

Most serial killers exhibit:

- **Antisocial Personality Disorder**: Lack of empathy and disregard for others' rights
- **Narcissism**: Inflated sense of self-importance and need for admiration
- **Psychopathy**: Shallow emotions, manipulativeness, and lack of remorse

## Developmental Factors

Many killers experience:

- **Childhood Abuse**: Physical, emotional, or sexual trauma
- **Attachment Disorders**: Inability to form healthy relationships
- **Fantasy Worlds**: Elaborate daydreams that escalate into reality

## Motivations and Patterns

Killers are often categorized by motivation:

- **Hedonistic**: Killing for pleasure or thrill
- **Power/Control**: Dominating victims for a sense of power
- **Mission-Oriented**: Believing they're "cleaning up" society
