# The Biology of Evil: Genetic and Neurological Factors

Evil isn't just a choice—it's often wired into our DNA and neural pathways. Recent studies reveal that certain genetic variations and brain abnormalities can predispose individuals to antisocial behavior.

## Genetic Predispositions

Research shows that variations in genes related to serotonin regulation and dopamine processing can influence aggressive tendencies. The MAOA gene, often called the "warrior gene," has been linked to increased risk of violent behavior under stress.

## Neurological Abnormalities

Brain scans of convicted criminals often show differences in:

- **Prefrontal Cortex**: Responsible for impulse control and decision-making
- **Amygdala**: Involved in fear and emotional processing
- **Hippocampus**: Linked to memory and emotional regulation

## Environmental Interactions

While biology lays the foundation, environmental factors can activate or suppress these tendencies. Childhood trauma, substance abuse, and social isolation often interact with genetic vulnerabilities.

## Implications for Society

Understanding these biological factors doesn't excuse harmful behavior but informs prevention strategies, rehabilitation programs, and early intervention.
